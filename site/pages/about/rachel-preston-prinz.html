Rachel Preston Prinz has spent 25 years studying and celebrating the craft of architecture and the Genius Loci - the Spirit of Place. Rachel is the founder of the hybrid architectural preservation and storytelling practice The Ministry of Architecture, whose projects have included historic site documentation, historic structures reports, national register nominations, and developing community engagement pieces about architecture, acequia, and trail projects. Rachel also documents the work of mid-century modern designer Alexander Girard in New Mexico.


Rachel is known for her TEDx and Pecha Kucha talks about architecture and place in New Mexico and beyond, as well as talks for the Creative Placemaking Leadership Summit, AIA-Albuquerque, AIA-Santa Fe, AIA-New Mexico, and the national AIA Women’s Leadership Summit. She has served as a preservation commissioner in Taos, the host of the UNM-Taos Sustainability Institute, co-host of TEDxABQWomen, and is an award-winning author, filmmaker, and dot connector working to confront New Mexico’s architectural mythology, as well as celebrate our thousand year tradition of sustainable design.

Rachel’s workRachel’s work has been featured on HGTV, Bravo, NMPBS, and Canadian PBS; in Reader’s Digest, Sunset, Trend, THE, Fine Art Connoisseur, and Inc. magazines. She’s been featured in numerous articles, talks, podcasts, interviews and books on design, leadership, and finding courage in difficult circumstances.

Rachel is an expert people connector and "sparkplug" who brings a new and exciting energy to everything she does.

And she's going blind with macular degeneration.
